about

Margot Tenenbaum Interiors is a studio made up of designers passionate about creating the perfect space for each client. Our interiors are timeless, reflecting our client’s personality, not the latest trend. This insight can only be delivered due to the personal relationship which Margot develops with each client. Attention to detail is key to creating a beautiful and truly personal space, which is why we only work with the highest quality materials and seek out the most skilled craftsmen for our projects.

margot

Margot Helen Tenenbaum was adopted at the age of two. She was a successful playwright and was awarded the Braverman grant for her work in the ninth grade. Margot is known for her extreme secrecy- none of her family know she is a smoker, or of her first marriage and divorce to a Jamaican recording artist at the age of twenty-two.

After her marriage to the prominent neurologist, Raleigh St. Clair, Margot decided to change careers and become an interior designer. Her new career builds on skills she developed designing sets for her plays and retains a theatrical and stylistic quality, true to its origins. Margot’s work is characterised by a whimsical use of colour and her eclectic style is often imitated, but rarely matched. Her skill lies in combining bright colours, bold patterns and exotic antiques to create a unique space tailor made for her clients.
